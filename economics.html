The Noble Prize In Economics


The Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences, officially the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel, is an economics award funded by Sveriges Riksbank and administered by the Nobel Foundation.


Claudia Goldin


Claudia Goldin was born on November 27, 1946. Therefore, as of that date, she would have been 75 years old.Claudia Goldin is the Henry Lee Professor of Economics at Harvard University. She has held various prestigious positions throughout her career, including serving as the president of the American Economic Association. Goldin has made significant contributions to the field of economics, particularly in the areas of labor markets.

Philip H. Dybvig


Philip H. Dybvig/Age 68 years May 22, 1955.Philip H. Dybvig is a professor at the Washington University in St. Louis, Olin Business School. His research interests include banking, financial crises, and financial intermediation. Dybvig has made notable contributions to the understanding of bank runs and deposit insurance, particularly through his influential work, "Banking and Liquidity."Philip H. Dybvig earned his Ph.D. in economics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

David Card


David Card was born on August 24, 1956. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 65 years old.David Card is the Class of 1950 Professor of Economics at the University of California, Berkeley. He has held various academic positions and is renowned for his research in labor economics, particularly his work on minimum wage impacts and immigration. Card has been influential in challenging traditional economic viewpoints through empirical studies and evidence-based research.

Robert B. Wilson


Wilson was born on May 16, 1937. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 84 years old.Robert B. Wilson is a professor at Stanford University. His research has primarily focused on game theory and auction theory. He has made significant contributions to understanding strategic interactions in various economic contexts, with a particular emphasis on auctions.Robert B. Wilson earned his Ph.D. in economics from Harvard University in 1963.

Michael Kremer


Michael Kremer was born on November 12, 1964. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 57 years old.Michael Kremer is the Gates Professor of Developing Societies in the Department of Economics at Harvard University. He has been influential in the field of development economics, particularly for his research and work on the economics of education, health, and agriculture in developing countries.Michael Kremer earned his B.A. in social studies from Harvard University in P.H.D.

Paul Michael Romer


Paul Michael Romer was born on November 6, 1955. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 66 years old.Paul Romer is an economist and policymaker. He has held various academic positions and has also been involved in policy work. Romer served as the Chief Economist and Senior Vice President of the World Bank from 2016 to 2018.Paul Romer earned his Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from the University of Chicago in 1977.

Richard Thaler


Richard H. Thaler was born on September 12, 1945. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 76 years old.Richard Thaler is the Charles R. Walgreen Distinguished Service Professor of Behavioral Science and Economics at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business. He has been a prominent figure in the field of behavioral economics, exploring how psychological factors influence economic decisions and challenging the traditional economic assumption of rational.

Oliver Hart


Oliver Hart was born on October 9, 1948. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 73 years old.Oliver Hart is the Andrew E. Furer Professor of Economics at Harvard University. He has held various academic positions throughout his career and has been a prominent figure in the field of contract theory, studying how contracts and agreements facilitate economic transactions.Oliver Hart earned his Bachelor of Arts degree in economics from King's College.

Angus Deaton


Angus Deaton was born on October 19, 1945. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 76 years old.Angus Deaton is the Dwight D. Eisenhower Professor of Economics and International Affairs Emeritus at Princeton University. He has made significant contributions to the fields of development economics, health economics, and microeconomics. Deaton has been involved in research on poverty, inequality, and consumption patterns around the world.

Jean Tirole


Jean Tirole was born on August 9, 1953. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 68 years old.In 2014, Jean Tirole was awarded the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel, often referred to as the Nobel Prize in Economics. He received this prestigious honor for his analysis of market power and regulation. Tirole's work has provided important insights into understanding the dynamics of industries, market structures, and the design of regulatory institutions.

Lars Peter Hansen


Lars Peter Hansen was born on October 26, 1952. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 69 years old.Lars Peter Hansen is the David Rockefeller Distinguished Service Professor in Economics, Statistics, and the College at the University of Chicago. He has held various academic positions and has been a prominent figure in the field of economics, particularly in financial econometrics and macroeconomics.

Lloyd Shapley


Lloyd Stowell Shapley was born on June 2, 1923, and he passed away on March 12, 2016. Therefore, at the time of his death, he was 92 years old.Lloyd Shapley held various academic positions during his career. He was a professor at several institutions, including Princeton University, Stanford University, and the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). He made substantial contributions to game theory, focusing on cooperative games, market design, and the concept of the Shapley value.

Thomas J. Sargent


Thomas J. Sargent was born on July 19, 1943, as of my last knowledge update in January 2022. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 78 years old.Thomas J. Sargent is the W.R. Berkley Professor of Economics and Business at New York University. He has held various academic positions throughout his career, including at the University of Minnesota and Stanford University. Sargent has made significant contributions to the understanding of macroeconomic dynamics.

Dale T. Mortensen


Dale T. Mortensen was born on February 2, 1939, and he passed away on January 9, 2014. Therefore, at the time of his death, he was 74 years old.Dale T. Mortensen had a distinguished career as an economist. He held various academic positions, including professorships at institutions such as Northwestern University and Aarhus School of Business. His research focused on understanding the dynamics of labor markets, unemployment, and the role of search frictions in shaping employment.

Oliver E. Williamson


Oliver E. Williamson was born on September 27, 1932, and he passed away on May 21, 2020. Therefore, at the time of his death, he was 87 years old.Oliver E. Williamson held various academic positions during his career. He was a professor of economics and law at the University of California, Berkeley. Williamson's work, particularly in the area of transaction cost economics, has had a profound impact on the understanding of economic organizations, contracts, and the boundaries of firms.

Paul Krugman


Paul Krugman was born on February 28, 1953. Therefore, as of that date, he would have been 68 years old.Paul Krugman is a Distinguished Professor of Economics at the Graduate Center of the City University of New York (CUNY) and a professor at the London School of Economics. He has had a prolific career in academia and is widely recognized for his contributions to economic theory and policy analysis. Krugman has also been a prominent columnist, author.
